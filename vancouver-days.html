Vancouver Days
Days like these. Summer smoke pickled our Vancouver sun cordial red. Visitations were regular: we tumbled the sky off kayak paddles and when our socks became cucumbers, we were almost Him, walking on water. When we were in need of a prophecy we offered chocolate to the microwave. It was most accurate when it was committing arson. These days we are always leaving and returning well-read in Daoist thought. At college in America a butterfly told you a hundred happy years are in a hundred-layered cake so now we must make it. We are following the recipe they gave you but our cat ate the measuring cup so now our batter calcifies like pumice and our crepes are Moroccan when we wanted them to be French. We will die young now. You tell me that Lady M closes at seven and if my semiconductor ETF goes up and we are swift about things then perhaps the Chinese spirits will watch over us still. We will do anything for a blessing now.
