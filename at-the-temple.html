at the temple
you have brought me to the temple and have found me incensing my laurels. the monk is sliding enlightenment onto my left wrist and you imagine me sinking into the peach tree wood of this beaded liberation. Maitreya giggles. he is preaching emptiness but I have sculpted him full-bellied, as always I am circular, my thoughts as closed-form as my bracelet. but still, you preach — and how can I intrude the steadiness of your speech to tell you that serenity is nauseating and constancy is kibbling and peace, a promise that would unravel the assorted paraphernalia I have so assiduously adorned to the shelves of my stomach lining? today, I can eject no more from my mouth than the slobber of a dry heave but still, you find solace in the apparition of my vomit, surrendered to this temple floor.
