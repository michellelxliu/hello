Film of Mildew
It is the season of nature's shaving. Autumn's dregs are alit by a liminal burn, and I am minding my minds behind a film of mildew. There she goes, my precocious daughter, skipping every second stair on the spiral, riposting every flare of the sky-boat as she journeys behind the leaves she burns silver into spikes for her crown. Here he comes, my prodigal son, cross-legged and crying the beguiling letters of his spine out my mouth. And now they are turning to me, still under the light on this library ledge to whisper sweet idyll, an obliging nothing: With me are novelty and apocalypse, crimes of our time, but behind this film a flickering moon is blurred full.
